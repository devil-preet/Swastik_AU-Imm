Employer Nomination Scheme (subclass 186) TRT

This visa is for skilled workers for Permanent Residency. It is applicable to both workers living inside and outside Australia.

Purpose:

♦ Stay and do a job in Australia indefinitely.
♦ Entitled to enroll in a study course for an indefinite period.
♦ Enroll for Medicare (Australia’s public healthcare scheme).
♦ Eligibility to apply for Australian citizenship.
♦ Travel to and fro from Australia for 5 years.
♦ Sponsor eligible relatives to come to Australia.
♦ Visa is granted in a particular skill as opted by the holder, but once granted, it is unnecessary to find employment or be employed in the same occupation.

Duration of Stay:

♦ As it is a permanent visa, you can stay for an indefinite time.

Streams of Subclass 186 Visa:

Temporary Residence Transition (TRT) Stream:

Criteria:

♦ To be eligible for TRT stream you must:
• Hold a Subclass 457 visa, a 482 visa, or a bridging visa.

There are 2 classes of applicants who can apply through TRT Stream:

♦ Applicants who applied for or held a 457 visa before 18 April 2017:
• If above is the case, Applicant can apply for 186 visa once he/she has worked with a sponsor for at least 2 years while the holder of this visa, on the basis of grandfathering arrangements set out by Home Affairs Department.
• Applicant must not be over 50 years of age.
♦ Applicants who applied for or held a 457 or 482 visa after 18 April 2017:
• If above is the case, applicant must have an occupation in the MLTSSL and can then apply for 186 visa once have worked for 3 years with your sponsor while the holder of this visa.
• Applicants of this class cannot apply for 186 visa if they have an occupation on the STSOL unless in a regional area and have worked for the employer (nominating one) for 3 years while the holder of a 457 or 482 visa.
• Applicant in this case must not be older than 45 years of age.
♦ Note: TRT stream applicants must prove that they have withdrawn the salary nominated in their 457/482 nomination in each relevant year.

Direct Entry Stream (DE):

♦ As the name indicates, this stream permits employers directly nominate their workers for permanent residence without negotiating a labor Agreement.
♦ Applicant need not hold any other visa first.

Criteria for DE stream:

♦ To be eligible for DE stream you must:
• Be inshore or offshore of Australia.
♦ Two stages to Direct Entry Process: • Employer will first lodge a nomination application indicating the position and the person they are nominating.
• Then, Employer lodges a visa application.
• Both the nomination and visa application can be lodged on the same day, but consideration will be given to nomination first, and its approval is required before the approval of visa application.

Labour Agreement Stream:

♦ It is for applicants who are sponsored by an employer through a labour agreement.

Eligibility of Business:

♦ It must be registered in Australia.
♦ It should have enough funds to pay for further 2 years to the employee.
♦ Turnover requirement must be fulfilled (depends on the position nominated by the business).
♦ For 186 visa TRT Stream Labour Marketing Testing is not required.
♦ Business must nominate the position for a minimum of 2 years (with an option to extend).

Eligibility Criteria:

♦ Applicant must be nominated by an Australian Employer.
♦ Occupation must be there on the 186 Visa Occupations List.
♦ Applicant must meet the Health and Character needs. Family members applying for the visa must qualify health and character requirements of Australian Government.
♦ Family members, even if not migrating to Australia, might also have to qualify character requirements.
♦ Have paid Outstanding debt (if any) to the Australian Government: If applicant or any family member of the applicant owes any debt to the Australian Government, it must be paid back or at least have formal arrangements to pay it back.
♦ Australian Values Statement: You must sign the Australian values statement, which assures that you will abide by all Australian laws and will respect their way of living.

Obligations:

♦ It is necessary that you and your family abide by all laws of the Australian Government.
♦ It is expected that the applicant will work for the nominating employer for a minimum of 2 years.
♦ You have to start your employment within the period of 6 months of:
• Entry to Australia (if the visa was granted while offshore).
• The grant date of visa (if the visa was granted while you are inshore).
♦ As a Main Applicant, you need to show that you possess Competent English unless excused. There is no exemption to the English language requirement from 16 November 2019.

Family:

♦ Members who can be enclosed in your application:
• Partner
• Dependent child or stepchild.
• Dependent child or stepchild of your partner.
• Dependent child or stepchild of you and your spouse’s dependent child or stepchild
♦ Applicant can include a member of the family unit in the application while and after applying but before deciding on your visa application.
♦ You can include someone who holds a subclass 457 or subclass 482 visa.

Cost:

♦ AUD$4,045–Main Applicant.
♦ Additional charges are there for other family applicants applying.

Second Installment:

♦ Family members having age above 18 years or above need to pay Second Installment, those who:
• Applies with the applicant for this visa.
• Has less than Functional English.
♦ Second Installment is AUD4,890
♦ Note: As a Main Applicant, you need to show that you possess Competent English unless excused. If exempt from showing competent English, show that you have Functional English. If you failed to prove you have Functional English, then you have to pay a second installment of AUD 9,800 whenever asked.
♦ Other costs include those of health checks, police certificates, and biometrics.

Place to Apply from:

♦ Applicant may apply this visa from onshore or offshore.
♦ If you are onshore, you must hold a substantive or a related bridging visa A, B, or C.
♦ In case of No Further Stay Condition on Substantive visa, still you might file an application in certain situations.
♦ Applicant can be inside or outside Australia at the time of deciding on the application.

Processing Time:

♦ Applications might take a longer time to process if not filled correctly, all documents are not provided, or correct visa application charges are not paid.

Condition(s) attached with Employer Nomination Scheme Subclass 186:

♦ 8515– Must not marry or enter into a de facto relationship before entering Australia.

Employer Nomination Direct Entry (subclass 186)

As the name indicates, this stream permits employers to directly nominate their workers for permanent residence without negotiating a labor agreement.

Two stages to Direct Entry Process:

♦ Employer will first lodge a nomination application indicating the position and the person they are nominating.
♦ Then, Employer lodges a visa application. Both the nomination and visa application can be lodged on the same day, but consideration will be given to nomination first, and its approval is required before the approval of the visa application.

This visa permits you:

♦ Stay permanently in Australia.
♦ Study or work in Australia.
♦ Enroll for Medicare (Australia’s public healthcare scheme).
♦ Sponsor your relatives to come to Australia.
♦ Travel: Travel inside or outside Australia for 5 years. In case you want to travel after the initial 5 years' travel facility, you will have to apply for and be granted a Resident-Return (RRV) to re-enter Australia as a permanent resident. If you become an Australian citizen then you don’t require a visa to enter Australia and can use it to travel to and from Australia. If you leave Australia after the travel facility date has expired, without having another permanent visa with having a valid travel facility, for instance, a RRV, you might not be able to return to Australia as a Permanent Resident.
♦ Apply for citizenship (if eligible).
♦ Newly arrived residents may have to wait to access benefits of Australian Government.

Duration of Stay:

♦ As it is a permanent visa, you can stay for an indefinite time.
♦ Your permanent residence starts on the day of the grant of the visa.

Family:

♦ Family members must be included any time of the lodge of your application but before deciding of the application.
♦ Family members, applying for the visa must qualify health and character requirements of the Australian Government.
♦ Members who can be enclosed in your application:
• Partner
• Dependent child or stepchild.
• Dependent child or stepchild of your partner.
• Dependent child or stepchild of you and your spouse’s dependent child or stepchild
♦ You can include someone who holds a subclass 457 or subclass 482 visa.

Cost:

♦ AUD$4,045–Main Applicant.
♦ Additional charges are there for other family applicants applying.

Second Installment:

♦ Family members having age above 18 years or above need to pay Second Installment, those who:
• Apply with the applicant for this visa.
• Have less than Functional English.
♦ Second Installment is AUD4,890
♦ Other costs include those of health checks, police certificates, and biometrics.

Place to Apply from:

♦ Applicant may apply for this visa from onshore or offshore.
♦ If you are onshore, you must hold a substantive or a related bridging visa A, B, or C.
♦ In the case of No Further Stay Condition on Substantive visa, still, you might file an application in certain situations.
♦ Applicant can be inside or outside Australia at the time of deciding on the application.

Processing Time:

♦ For 75% of applications: 6 Months
♦ For 90% of applications: 7 Months.

Eligibility Criteria:

♦ You need to have an occupation that is on the eligible skilled occupation list.
♦ You should have a minimum of 3 years of relevant work experience, unless exempted
♦ Own a positive skill assessment, unless exempted.
♦ Applicant must be nominated by an Australian Employer, whose business is lawfully and actively operating.
♦ You have to start your employment within the period of 6 months of:
• Entry to Australia (if the visa was granted while offshore).
• The grant date of visa (if the visa was granted while you are inshore).
♦ Applicant must meet the Health and Character needs. Family members, applying for the visa must qualify health and character requirements of the Australian Government.
♦ Family members, even if not migrating to Australia, might also have to qualify character requirements.
♦ Have paid outstanding debt (if any) to the Australian Government: If the applicant or any family member of the applicant owes any debt to the Australian Government, it must be paid back or at least have formal arrangements to pay it back.
♦ Australian Values Statement: You must sign the Australian values statement, which assures that you will abide by all Australian laws and will respect their way of living.
♦ As a Main Applicant, you need to show that you possess Competent English.
♦ It is necessary that you and your family abide by all laws of the Australian Government.
♦ It is expected that the applicant will work for the nominating employer for a minimum of 2 years.
♦ Usually, you must be under 45 years of age to apply for this visa.

Not be eligible:

♦ In case of Previous Cancelled Visa.

Condition(s) attached with Employer Nomination Scheme Subclass 186:

♦ 8515– Must not marry or enter into a de facto relationship before entering Australia.

Permanent Residence (Skilled Regional) visa (subclass 191)

This visa does not commence until 16 November 2022. It is for people who have resided, worked, and studied in a regional area in Australia on a previous eligible visa.

This visa permits you:

♦ Dwell in Australia permanently.
♦ Entitled to enroll in a study course or do a job anywhere in Australia.
♦ Eligibility to apply for Australian citizenship.
♦ Sponsor eligible relatives to come to Australia.

My Eligibility Criteria:

♦ You need to hold an eligible regional provisional 491 or 494 visa when applying for the Subclass 191 Visa.
♦ You must have lived for a minimum of 3 years and have a taxable income at or above a particular income threshold for a minimum of 3 years.
♦ Applicants (Primary and secondary) have to comply with the conditions of the qualified visa you hold or have held.

Duration of Stay:

♦ As it is a permanent visa, you can stay for an indefinite time.
♦ Your permanent residence starts on the day of the grant of the visa for citizenship.

Travel:

♦ This visa has a travel component, expiry of which is 5 years after the grant date of the visa.

Family:

♦ If family members hold an eligible visa, they can be added to the application too.
♦ Family members must be included at the time of the lodge of your application but not after the submission of the application.
♦ Family members who no longer fulfill the standard definition of family members can also be included provided they hold an eligible visa that was granted on the fact that they were a member of your family unit (for example, a child in your application who has turned 23 years of age since they were granted an eligible visa).
♦ Family members, applying for the visa must qualify health and character requirements of the Australian Government.
♦ Family members, even if not migrating to Australia, might also have to qualify health and character requirements.

Cost:

♦ For the main Applicant, there is a base charge.
♦ Announcement of base charge will be done near to the commencement date.
♦ Additional charges are there for other family applicants applying.
♦ Second Installment: Family members having age 18 years or above need to pay the Second Installment, those who:
• Apply with the applicant for this visa.
• Have less than Functional English.
• Pay the second installment only when asked to. It is charged only if the visa is going to be granted.
♦ Second installment amount will be announced near to the commencement date.
♦ Other costs include those of health checks, police certificates, and biometrics.

Places to Apply From:

♦ You must be inside Australia when you apply and during the grant of the visa.

Processing Time:

♦ Applications are assessed on a case-by-case basis.
♦ Processing time depends on individual situations also.
♦ Applications might take a longer time to process if not filled correctly, all documents are not provided, it takes time by us for verification of your information, and also, the application can’t be processed if the correct visa application charges are not paid.

Conditions for Skilled Regional (Permanent Residence) Visa (Subclass 191):

♦ You need to hold an eligible regional provisional 491 or 494 visa when applying for Subclass 191 Visa.
♦ You must have lived for a minimum of 3 years and have a taxable income at or above a particular income threshold for a minimum of 3 years.
♦ Applicants (Primary and secondary) have to comply with the conditions of the qualified visa you hold or have held.

Distinguished Talent visa Offshore (subclass 124)

This visa permits you:

♦ Stay permanently in Australia.
♦ Study or work in Australia.
♦ Enroll for Medicare (Australia’s public healthcare scheme).
♦ Sponsor your relatives to come to Australia.
♦ Apply for citizenship (if eligible).

Duration of Stay:

♦ As it is a permanent visa, you can stay for an indefinite time.
♦ Your permanent residence starts on the day of the grant of the visa.

Eligibility Criteria:

♦ Nomination: Applicant must be nominated, and the nominator should also meet criteria set by the Australian Government.
♦ Applicant must meet the Health and Character needs.
♦ Place to Lodge the Application from:
- You can be inside or outside Australia but not in immigration clearance when you apply for this visa.
- You must be offshore for your visa to be granted.
♦ You need to have an exceptional and outstanding achievement as an internationally recognized record in one of the below areas:
- A sport
- A profession
- Arts
- Academic and research
♦ You must be nominated with a national reputation in relation to your area of talent by an Australian citizen, Australian permanent resident, eligible citizen of New Zealand, or organization of Australia.

Obligations:

♦ It is necessary that you and your family abide by all laws of the Australian Government.
♦ It is expected from you that you will contribute to Australia in your field of expertise.
♦ Have paid Outstanding debt (if any) to the Australian Government: If the applicant or any family member of the applicant owes any debt to the Australian Government, it must be paid back.
♦ Australian Values Statement: You must sign the Australian values statement, which assures that you will abide by all Australian laws and will respect their way of living.
♦ Applicants of age 18 or above need to have Functional English; otherwise, you will have to pay an extra charge, called the second installment for this visa to be granted.

Family:

♦ Family members must be included any time of the lodge of your application but before deciding on the application.
♦ If the applicant is under 18 years of age, you can include:
- A parent
- Members of the family unit of that parent.
♦ Family members, applying for the visa must qualify health and character requirements of the Australian Government.

Not be eligible:

♦ In case of Previous Cancelled Visa.

Cost:

♦ For the main Applicant, the visa cost is AUD4,110.
♦ Additional charges are there for other family applicants applying.
♦ Also, the application can’t be processed if correct visa application charges are not paid.
♦ Family members having age 18 years or above need to pay Second Installment, those who:
- Apply with the applicant for this visa.
- Has less than Functional English.
- Second installment cost is AUD4,890.

Processing Time:

♦ Applications are assessed on a case-by-case basis.
♦ Processing time depends on individual situations also.
♦ Applications might take a longer time to process if not filled correctly, all documents are not provided, or it takes time by us for verification of your information. Also, the application can’t be processed if correct visa application charges are not paid.

Steps Involved (in Applying):

♦ Step 1. Before Applying: – Check your Passport and health examinations.
♦ Step 2. Gather all Evidences: – Gather all required documents including identity documents, character documents, proof of prior relationships, record of achievement documents, nominator documents, etc.
♦ Step 3. Visa Application: – Apply online providing correct and relevant information.
♦ Step 4. After Applying: – Await confirmation from the Government of Australia, arrange travel, provide biometrics, and add family members to the application.
♦ Step 5. Outcome of Visa: – Await notification of visa outcome, which will be provided in writing.

Conditions attached with Distinguished Talent Visa (Subclass 124):

♦ 8502– You must not enter Australia before the person specified (usually the main visa holder or other relevant person such as your sponsor in the case of a Partner visa) in the visa has entered Australia.

Distinguished Talent visa Onshore (subclass 858)

This visa permits you:

♦ Stay permanently in Australia.
♦ Join a study course or work in Australia.
♦ Entitled to enroll for Medicare (Australia’s public healthcare scheme).
♦ Sponsor your eligible relatives to come to Australia.
♦ Travel: Travel inside or outside Australia for 5 years. In case you want to travel after the initial 5 years' travel facility, you will have to apply for and be granted a Resident-Return (RRV) to re-enter Australia as a permanent resident.
♦ Apply for citizenship (if eligible).

Duration of Stay:

♦ As it is a permanent visa, you can stay for an indefinite time.
♦ Your permanent residence starts on the day of the grant of the visa.

My Eligibility Criteria:

♦ Nomination: Applicant must be nominated, and the nominator should also meet the criteria of the Australia Government.
♦ Applicant must meet the Health and Character needs.
♦ Applicant need not hold a specific visa to be able to apply for this visa. However, if in Australia, then you need to hold either of the following:
• A substantive visa
• Bridging visa A
• Bridging visa B
• Bridging visa C
♦ You can be of any age to apply for this visa.

Place to Lodge the Application from:

♦ You can be inside or outside Australia but not in immigration clearance when you apply for this visa and during the decision of the visa.
♦ You need to have an exceptional and outstanding achievement as an internationally recognized record in one of the below areas: • A sport • A profession • Arts • Academic and research
♦ You must be nominated by an Australian citizen, Australian permanent resident, eligible citizen of New Zealand, or organization of Australia having a national reputation in the same field as you.

Obligations:

♦ It is necessary that you and your family abide by all laws of the Australian Government.
♦ It is expected from you that you will contribute to Australia in your field of expertise.
♦ Have paid Outstanding debt (if any) to the Australian Government: If the applicant or any family member of the applicant owes any debt to the Australian Government, it must be paid back.
♦ Australian Values Statement: You must sign the Australian values statement, which assures that you will abide by all Australian laws and will respect their way of living.
♦ Applicants of age 18 or above need to have Functional English; otherwise, you will have to pay an extra charge, called the second installment for this visa to be granted.

Family:

♦ Family members can be included in the application when you apply or at any time of the lodge of your application but not before the decision of the application.
♦ If the applicant is under 18 years of age, you can include: • A parent • Members of the family unit of that parent.
♦ Family members, applying for the visa must qualify health and character requirements of the Australian Government.

Not be eligible:

♦ In case of Previous Cancelled Visa.

Cost:

♦ For the main Applicant, the visa cost is AUD4,110.
♦ Additional charges are there for other family applicants applying.
♦ Also, the application can’t be processed if correct visa application charges are not paid.
♦ Family members having age 18 years or above need to pay Second Installment, those who: • Apply with the applicant for this visa. • Has less than Functional English. • Second installment cost is AUD4,890.

Processing Time:

♦ Applications are assessed on a case-by-case basis.
♦ Processing time depends on individual situations also, such as if a complete application is lodged or not with all required documents, how fast is the response to any request for additional information, how much duration is required to check the supporting information, how long it takes to obtain extra required information from external agencies (related to health, character, and national security requirements).
♦ Applications might take a longer time to process if not filled correctly, all documents are not provided, it takes time by us for verification of your information, and also, the application can’t be processed if correct visa application charges are not paid.

Steps Involved (in Applying):

Step 1. Before Applying:

• Check your Passport.
• You might need to give health examinations before applying or you can wait until told to do so.
• If you need further help in the application process, contact Visazz Group.

Step 2. Gather all Evidences:

• Documents: Identity Documents, National Identity card (if you own), Character documents, Proof of name change (Certificate of Marriage or Divorce, Name change documents from Australian registry of Births, deaths and Marriages or the applicable overseas Authority, Documents proving your other names by which you were known), Prior Relationships, Record of Achievement documents, Nominator Documents, Documents related to prove your ability to establish yourself in Australia, Character documents, Functional English documents, Partner Documents, Documents of dependents under 18 years of age.
• Identity proof, Proof of their relation with you (Birth or marriage certificate), Character documents (if apply), Parental Responsibility documents: You need to get approval for an applicant under 18 years of age to move to Australia from an individual who: Is not coming with the concerned child, Has a right to decide (legally) where the child lives. For that, they must complete: A statutory declaration that they are giving their consent to the child to migrate to Australia on this visa. Other options are to show: Order of Australian court allowing the child to migrate to Australia, The laws of your nation allowing the child to move to Australia.
• Documents of dependents over 18 years of age: Identity proof, Proof of their relation with you (Birth or marriage certificate), Character documents, Proof of dependency. Includes: Birth certificate or adoption papers, Proof of financial dependency for a minimum of 12 months before applying. For that provide: Proof of their current studying, Tax records of them, Proof that they live with you.
• Dependents above 18 must be: Above 18 but not yet 23 years of age, Above 23 years yet unable to support themselves due to physical or cognitive limitation.
• Prepare your documents: You will need to translate all non-English documents into English, scan your documents or get them photographed in color, the scan must be a valid one, keep a replica of your completed application.

Step 3. Visa Application:

• Visa is applied online, by providing correct and relevant information.
• Consult our migration specialists for an easy-going process.

Step 4. After Applying:

• Government of Australia will inform you after receiving your application and documents.
• Travel: You need not to arrange the travel until your visa confirmation is not sent to you in writing.
• Biometrics: You will be let know in prior if there is need of Biometrics.
• Family unit: Members of the family can be added in application after applying but before the visa is decided.

Step 5. Outcome of Visa:

• While deciding on your Permanent Partner Visa, you can be onshore or offshore. Outcome of visa will be notified in written manner.
• During granting of your visa, we will inform you about your visa grant number, the date of start of your visa and its conditions.
• In case of refusal of our visa, we will let you know the reason of refusal and if you have a right to a review of the decision.
• Application fee is non- refundable.

• Check other conditions in your grant letter that you and accompanying family members have.
