Partner Provisional visa (subclass 309)

This Partner Visa permits the de facto Partner or Spouse of an Australian citizen, Permanent Resident of Australia, or an eligible citizen of New Zealand to dwell in Australia Temporarily. Obtaining this visa initiates the process of proceeding towards the Permanent Partner visa (Subclass 100).

Eligibility Criteria

You must be in a genuine relationship with your spouse or de facto partner who is a citizen of Australia, permanent resident of Australia, or an eligible citizen of New Zealand.

You need to be outside Australia at the time of applying for this visa and also at the time of grant of visa.

You and your family members must meet all visa conditions and obey Australian laws.

Visa Benefits

♦ Dwell and do job in Australia until decided upon Permanent Partner (Migrant) Visa (Subclass 100) or the application is withdrawn.
♦ Entitled to enroll in study course (Without Government Aid).
♦ Enroll for Medicare (Australia’s public healthcare scheme).
♦ Enroll in free English Language classes up to 510 hours given by the Adult Migrant English Program.
♦ Travel to and from Australia as persistently as you wish.

Duration of Stay

Applicants can stay until decided upon Permanent Partner (Migrant) Visa (100) application or the application is withdrawn. Usually, stay is from 15 to 24 months.

In some situations, including where you have been in a long-term relationship before you apply, you might not stay on the 309 visa at all. You might be granted the permanent visa immediately after grant of the temporary 309 visa.

Family Members

Members of the family unit can also be included in your application. You can include them when you lodge your visa application or add a dependent child after you lodge your application but before deciding on the applicant’s temporary visa.

Family members who apply with you must meet health and character requirements of Australian Government and be outside Australia.

Family members can also be added after granting of the visa.

Cost

For the main applicant: AUD7,715. This fee covers both this temporary visa and the permanent Partner (Migrant) visa (subclass 100).

Place to Apply from

Applicant must be outside Australia when applying for this visa and when deciding the applicant’s temporary visa application.

Processing Times

75% of applications: 17 months
90% of applications: 25 months

Application, however, might take a longer time to process if not filled correctly, all documents are not provided, or if verification of your information takes time.

Application cannot be processed if the correct visa application charges are not paid. If that is the case, you will be notified.

Travel

Applicant can travel inside or outside from Australia as many times as one wishes for. Note: Being the visa holder, the first entry to Australia must be made before the date mentioned in your visa grant letter. The first entry date is generally set at 12 months from the date of visa grant.

Conditions for Partner (Provisional) (Subclass 309) visa

♦ 8502: Applicant must not enter Australia before the person specified in the visa (Main visa holder or applicant’s sponsor in the case of partner visa) has entered Australia.
♦ 8515: Applicant must not marry or enter into a de facto relationship before entering Australia, otherwise the visa might get cancelled.

Partner Permanent visa (Subclass 100)

This Partner Visa allows the de facto Partner or Spouse of an Australian citizen, Permanent Resident of Australia, or an eligible citizen of New Zealand to migrate to Australia Permanently. This visa is granted to the applicant having Subclass 309 visa.

Visa Benefits

♦ Dwell, do job, or enroll in study course in Australia.
♦ Apply for Citizenship of Australia (if eligible).
♦ Sponsor family acquaintances (eligible) to migrate to Australia.
♦ Enroll for Medicare (Australia’s public healthcare scheme).
♦ Travel from and to Australia as many times as desired for 5 years from the grant of this visa.
♦ Ability to sponsor family acquaintances to come to Australia.
♦ Attend free English language classes given by The Adult Migrant English Program (if eligible).

Eligibility Criteria

Applicant must, in maximum of cases, have a Partner (Provisional) (Temporary) Subclass 309 visa or a Dependent child (Subclass 445) visa.

You must be in a genuine relationship with your spouse or de facto partner who is an Australian citizen (eligible), permanent resident of Australia, or an eligible citizen of New Zealand.

Usually, before applying for the Permanent visa it is required that a period of 2 years has been passed since the applicant has filed for combined Subclass 100 and Subclass 309 visa.

No matter what kind of relationship (either married or de facto), you must clarify:

♦ Your relationship is real and enduring.
♦ Your relationship is mutually beneficial.
♦ You live together, or you don’t live separately.

Cost

Cost gets paid at the time of combined application for the temporary and permanent partner visa.

Other payments might include that of health and police checks.

It is necessary that you and your family abide by all laws of Australian Government.

Visa label

Partner Permanent visa (Subclass 801)

This visa allows the spouse or de facto partner of a citizen of Australia, Australian Permanent Resident, or an eligible New Zealand citizen to immigrate permanently to Australia.

Visa Benefits

♦ Stay permanently in Australia.
♦ Study or work in Australia.
♦ Entitled to enrol for Medicare (Australia’s public healthcare scheme).
♦ Sponsor your family acquaintances to come to Australia.
♦ Apply for citizenship of Australia after 4 years (if eligible).
♦ Attend free English language classes given by The Adult Migrant English Program (if eligible).

Duration of Stay

As it is a permanent visa, you can stay for an indefinite time. Your permanent residence starts on the day of the grant of the visa for citizenship.

Family

Applicant can include his/her dependent child while applying for combined visas. If you hadn’t included your dependent child in your temporary visa application, you can add them still in your application before deciding on your permanent visa application. The child must be onshore and hold a dependent child visa (subclass 445).

Eligibility Criteria

Applicant must hold a (subclass 820) (temporary) Partner visa. You must continue to be in a relationship with your spouse or de facto partner unless your relationship has broken off under special situations. You must be legally married to an Australian citizen, permanent resident, or eligible New Zealand citizen. Applicants need to have been in a de facto relationship (if not married) for a minimum of 12 months before lodging the application unless you have registered your relationship or there are circumstances enough to waive the 12-month requirement.

No matter what kind of relationship (either married or de facto), you must clarify that your relationship is real and enduring, mutually beneficial, and you live together or don’t live separately. Abide by all Australian laws while on your temporary visa. You may need to provide extra documentation for processing of this visa after 2 years of submitting your temporary partner visa. Have paid outstanding debt (if any) to the Australian Government.

Cost

Paid at the time of combined application for the temporary and permanent partner visa.

Visa Subclass Fee Additional Applicant Fee (Child)
Subclass 820/801 visa $7,715 $1,935 (Under 18) $3,860 (Over 18)

Both visas are applied at once, and you have to pay one application charge only.

801 Onshore Partner visa (Phases)

Phase 1: First, lodge a combined subclass 820/801 visa application by paying a single lodgment fee. At first, you will be evaluated for the subclass 820 visa. If visa grant requirements are fulfilled, then you will be granted a temporary visa (which permits you to reside in Australia) while your subclass 801 Application is under process.

Phase 2: 2 years after lodgment of Subclass 820/801 visa, you will need to clarify that you still meet partner visa grant requirements.

Bridging Visa

While your 820 visa is under process, you will be granted a bridging visa. You will be permitted to enrol in Course of study and in Medicare.

You and Your Australian partner will need to provide as required with evidence given below:

♦ Identity Documents (Passport and Birth Certificate).
♦ Character documents.
♦ Certified Registration proof of marriage or relationship (if applicable).
♦ At least 2 forms 888’s (Statutory Declaration) given by witnesses attesting that your relationship is true.
♦ Each of you should provide a written statement or statutory declaration covering the history and development of your relationship.

After 2 years of lodging subclass 820/801 visa application, supply relevant documents to immigration. You don’t need to wait for the 2-year period instead send the documents 1 month before the time of 2 years. Documents include updated documents related to the previous ones, police clearances, relationship evidence from the period of lodgment of combined subclass 820/801 visa application to date.

Visa is applied online, by providing correct and relevant information.

In case of refusal of our visa, we will let you know the reason for refusal and if you have a right to a review of the decision. The application fee is non-refundable.

Prospective Marriage visa (subclass 300)

Do you have a partner living in Australia as a permanent resident or citizen? Are you looking for a pathway to live with your partner in Australia? Click here to know your eligibility! The applicant might be granted a Special Category visa(Subclass 444) which will cease their Prospective Marriage Visa.

Overview

Commonly known as a fiancé visa, this is a temporary visa that permits an applicant to travel to Australia to marry his/her partner who is an Australian citizen, permanent resident, or an eligible New Zealand citizen, within 9 months.

Key Features

♦ Move to Australia.
♦ Enrol in a study course at the applicant’s own expenditure.
♦ Travel freely.
♦ New Zealand Passport Holders: Passport holders of New Zealand need to let the immigration officer at the border know they have a Prospective Marriage visa. If not done, the Applicant might be granted a Special Category visa(Subclass 444) which will cease their Prospective Marriage Visa.

Duration of Stay

This is a temporary visa with a stay time of up to 9 months from the date of grant.

Eligibility Criteria

♦ Applicant must meet the Health and Character needs.
♦ Applicant's age should be 18 years old or above.
♦ Needs to have a sponsor.
♦ Should be the fiancé of the candidate.
♦ Shouldn’t have been a sponsor of more than one applicant in the past.
♦ Shouldn’t have been a spouse application sponsor in the previous 5 years.
♦ Should be of good character.

Obligations

It is necessary that you and your family abide by all laws of the Australian Government.

Your Marriage

It is required that Applicant must enter Australia before the date mentioned on the grant letter. You must have married your Prospective spouse sometime before the expiry of your Prospective Marriage visa. Marriage must be Substantial beneath Australian Law.

Cost

From AUD 7,715. Additional charges are there for other family applicants applying. Other costs include those of health checks, police certificates, and biometrics.

Applying Process

It consists of a 3-step process:

♦ At first, you need to lodge a prospective marriage visa (Subclass 300), which if successfully granted will permit you to travel to Australia to marry your Prospective spouse within 9 months.
♦ Once married, then Lodge a combined application for a temporary (Subclass 820) and a permanent visa (Subclass 801) at the same moment.
♦ After 2 years of Lodgment, you will then be assessed for the subclass 801 visa. If your application is successful, you will be granted PR in Australia.

Steps Involved (in Applying)

Step 1. Before Applying

Check your Passport. You might need to give health examinations before applying or you can wait until told to do so. If you need further help in the application process, contact Visazz Group.

Step 2. Gather all Evidence

Documents include Identity Documents, Character documents, Proof of name change, Your Relationship with Prospective Spouse, Prior Relationships, Character documents, Australian Police certificate, Overseas Police certificates, Documents of dependents under 18 years of age, Documents of dependents over 18 years of age, Prepare your documents.

Step 3. Visa Application

Visa is applied online, by providing correct and relevant information. Consult our migration specialists for an easy-going process.

Step 4. After Applying

The government of Australia will inform you after receiving your application and documents.

Processing Time

75% of applications: 17 months. 90% of applications: 20 months. Application, however, also might take a longer time to process if not filled correctly. All documents are not provided. It takes time for us to verification of your information. You need to give accurate visa charges so as to your application be processed.

Visa Label

Your visa will be digitally linked to your passport.

Conditions

Obtaining the visa (In Australia): You must abide by all Australian laws. Check your visa expiry and its conditions. You must enter Australia by the date as mentioned in your visa grant letter. Get married to your Prospective Spouse before the expiry of your Visa. Check other conditions in your grant letter that you and accompanying family members have. After granting this visa, for settling in Australia, you need to apply Partner visa (820 & 801) after your marriage and before the end of the prospective marriage visa.
