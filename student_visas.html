Visa Eligibility Criteria:

You must have a valid student visa.

You can only include your partner and children.

All family member details must be declared in the student visa* application or if the applicant became a member of the family unit of the primary person before the grant of the student visa to the primary person, then his/her information must be provided in the primary applicant’s student visa application.

For children to be included in the application they must be unmarried and under 18 years old.

Your dependents must hold and provide a valid Overseas student health cover (OSHC) for your entire visa tenure.

You must meet the financial requirements. You must demonstrate sufficient access to funds for one year including your family. Use of Student visa financial calculator.

Your dependents must meet the Genuine temporary entrant criteria. Our qualified team can assist you in writing the statement.

Your dependents must undertake the visa medical examinations in the last 12 months prior to the lodgement of the visa application.

Your dependents must meet the character requirements.

*If you failed to disclose your family member’s then the department may refuse to grant them visas.

Visa Conditions

8201 – Maximum three months study: This condition means that you are only allowed to engage in up to but not exceeding three months of study or training while you are in Australia.

8104 – Work limitation: This condition means that you must not engage in work until the primary student visa holder has commenced a course of study. You are also not permitted to work for more than 40 hours a fortnight while you are in Australia. If the course of study the primary student visa holder undertakes is for a master’s or doctoral degree, then you are permitted to work more than 40 hours per fortnight but only in these circumstances.

8501 – Maintain health insurance: This condition means that you must maintain adequate arrangements for health insurance (generally Overseas Student Health Cover) while you are in Australia. You must not enter Australia before your Overseas Student Health Cover has begun. You are not permitted to remain in Australia after your Overseas Student Health Cover has expired. If you are a student from Norway, Belgium, or Sweden you may not need to maintain Overseas Student Health Cover.

8516 – Must maintain eligibility: This condition means that you must continue to maintain your eligibility as a person who would satisfy the criteria for the grant of your visa.

Bridging visa conditions

♦ Work Limitation

♦ (1A) The holder must not engage in any work in Australia before the holder’s course of study commences.

♦ Subject to subclause (2), the holder must not engage in work in Australia for more than 40 hours a fortnight during any fortnight when the holder’s course of study or training is in session.

(a) to work that was specified as a requirement of the course when the course particulars were entered in the Commonwealth Register of Institutions and Courses for Overseas Students; and

Student Guardian Visa Subclass 590

This visa generally allows you to take care of a student who is under 18 years of age. You can provide assistance to more than 1 student at a time.

Once the visa is granted, you can stay in Australia till the date specified on your visa grant letter. If you wish to extend your stay, you can always apply for a further Student Guardian Visa.

You cannot bring any family members under 6 years of age. If your family member is over 6 years, they need to apply for their own student visa.

Visa conditions:

♦ 8101 – No work

You must not work in Australia. This means when in Australia, you must not do work that a person would normally get paid for.

♦ 8201 – Maximum 3 months study

You can study up to 20 hours a week on an English Language Intensive Course for Overseas Students (ELICOS).

♦ 8501 – Maintain adequate health insurance

You must have and maintain adequate health insurance for the whole of your stay in Australia.

♦ 8502 – Not arrive before person specified in the visa

You must not enter Australia before the person specified in the visa has entered Australia. This is usually the main visa holder or another relevant person such as your sponsor in the case of a Partner visa.

♦ 8516 – Continue to satisfy the criteria for the grant of the visa

You must continue to satisfy the criteria for the grant of the visa.

♦ 8534 – No further stay

While you are in Australia, we will not grant you a new substantive visa, other than:

♦ a Subclass 485 (Temporary Graduate) visa, or

♦ a Subclass 590 (Student Guardian) visa, or

♦ a Protection visa

♦ 8537 – Live with the student and provide for their accommodation, support and welfare

You must live in Australia while the nominating student is in Australia. While you and the nominating student are in Australia, you must:

♦ stay with them

♦ provide them with appropriate accommodation

♦ support them

♦ provide for their general welfare

♦ 8538 – Not leave Australia without the student without our approval

You must not leave Australia without the student without our approval. If you need to leave Australia without the student you are caring for (the nominating student), you must first prove to us:

♦ there are compassionate and compelling reasons for leaving Australia without the student

♦ you have made alternative arrangements for the student’s accommodation, support and general welfare

Training Visa (Subclass 407)

If you need a professional job in well-known companies, you need to develop skills or abilities to improve your statistics of academic or professional career experience. Do you want this?

Do you want to increase your practical talent in your existing employment?

Then check out Australia! Apply for the Training Visa (Subclass 407) to take a chance to be skilled enough and workplace ready!

How Does a Training Visa Help?

A training visa allows you to visit Australia for some duration and join in occupational training or professional development.

You can take place either of the following types of occupational training under 407 a visa:

♦ Workplace-based training to acquire registration or licensing: This training covers the people who require registration, membership, or licensing to start an occupation in their home country of Australia.
♦ Structured Workplace-Based training to skills: Training that covers the people who are selected by verified temporary activities sponsor in Australia for an occupation described in the list of eligible skilled trades but needs undergoing designed workplace-based training to improve skills for that field.
♦ Training that sharpens the skills or enhances competency: This training is split into three categories:
- Overseas Qualification
- Government Support
- Professional Development

Benefits Of The Training Visa 407

The training visa allows you to:

♦ Apply for the visa both onshore and offshore
♦ Live in Australia for up to 2 years and complete the training specified in your application
♦ Leave and re-enter Australia frequently until the pass is valid
♦ Bring eligible family members to accompany you to Australia

Eligibility Criteria for Visa Applicants, Sponsors & Nominators

To be eligible for this visa, applicants must:

♦ They must be at least 18 years of age (unless you are exempt)
♦ An approved sponsor must sponsor them to participate in a professional development program or occupational training.
♦ They must be nominated for an approved occupation by the Australian organization if it is not a Commonwealth agency.
♦ They must be invited to apply for the visa if the sponsor is a Commonwealth agency.
♦ They get occupational training directly from the sponsor unless an exemption applies.
♦ They lodge an application before 18th May 2017 for existing sponsorship by an approved training and research and professional development sponsor.
♦ They only participate in the occupational training that meets the criteria for one of the nomination eligibility types.
♦ They must be proficient in functional English to acquire training and meet occupational health and safety standards.
♦ They have a genuine intention to stay in Australia temporarily.
♦ They should meet health and character requirements.
♦ They have sufficient financial backup and arrangements for health insurance for themselves and accompanying family members for the duration of their stay in Australia.
♦ They should meet all other requirements specified for your training program.

Those sponsoring someone on a 407 visa must:

♦ It would help if you were an organisation operating lawfully in Australia, a government agency or a foreign government agency.
♦ You will provide the occupational training directly unless an exemption applies.
♦ You should have no unfavourable information identified about you or any other person associated with you unless it is reasoned for the Immigration Department to ignore
♦ You should have the ability to comply with all the sponsorship obligations.

To nominate someone on a 407 visa:

♦ You must be an approved temporary activity sponsor or
♦ You have applied to become an approved temporary activity sponsor
♦ The nomination will not be considered unless the sponsorship is approved.

Family Members to Include in the Visa Application

You can include the following family members in your visa application:

♦ Partner (Married or de facto)
♦ Your or your partner’s dependent child (up to 23 years of age)

You must have evidence to support a claimed family relationship. You must also have a written statement, including approval from your sponsor to sponsor your family members.

Financial Requirements

You must provide evidence of your financial capacity to support yourself and accompanying family members for the duration of your stay in Australia.

You and all the dependent family members are required to:

♦ Undergo medical examinations within 12 months of lodging a visa application
♦ Obtain police clearance from each country (you and family members) have lived in for 12 months or more in the past ten years

Health Insurance

You and dependent family members must be covered by Australia’s national health scheme (Medicare) unless your country has a reciprocal health agreement in Australia. The health insurer can either be in Australia or your home.
